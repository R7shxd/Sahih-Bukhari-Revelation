Modalities of Wahy: Interactive Guide

Divine revelation to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) manifested in various forms, each with unique characteristics and impact. Explore each modality below.

✨

Good Dreams (الرؤيا الصالحة)

This was the very first form of Divine Inspiration Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) received. These dreams were clear, vivid, and always came true, serving as a gentle and preparatory introduction to the profound experience of Wahy.

Prophet's Experience & Quote:

"The first form of Divine Inspiration which Allah's Messenger (PBUH) received was true dreams which used to come true like bright daylight."

أَوَّلُ مَا بُدِئَ بِهِ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم مِنَ الْوَحْىِ الرُّؤْيَا الصَّالِحَةُ فِي النَّوْمِ، فَكَانَ لاَ يَرَى رُؤْيَا إِلاَّ جَاءَتْ مِثْلَ فَلَقِ الصُّبْحِ

(Sahih al-Bukhari)

🔔

Ringing of a Bell (صلصلة الجرس)

This was described by the Prophet (PBUH) as the hardest and most intense form of revelation. It was accompanied by a profound auditory and sensory experience, placing an immense spiritual and physical burden upon him.

Prophet's Experience & Quote:

"Sometimes it is (revealed) like the ringing of a bell, this form of Inspiration is the hardest of all and then this state passes away after I have grasped what is inspired. Sometimes the Angel comes in the form of a man and talks to me and I grasp all that he says."

أَحْيَانًا يَأْتِينِي مِثْلَ صَلْصَلَةِ الْجَرَسِ، وَهُوَ أَشَدُّهُ عَلَىَّ، فَيُفْصَمُ عَنِّي وَقَدْ وَعَيْتُ عَنْهُ مَا قَالَ، وَأَحْيَانًا يَتَمَثَّلُ لِي الْمَلَكُ رَجُلاً فَيُكَلِّمُنِي فَأَعِي مَا يَقُولُ

(Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), Sahih al-Bukhari)

The Prophet (PBUH) would often sweat profusely even on a very cold day during this type of revelation, indicating its immense physical toll.

👤

Angel in Human Form

In this modality, Angel Gabriel would appear in the form of a man and engage in direct conversation with Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). This form allowed for clearer comprehension and was generally a more accessible way for the Prophet to receive the divine message.

Prophet's Experience & Quote:

"Sometimes the Angel comes in the form of a man and talks to me and I grasp all that he says."

وَأَحْيَانًا يَتَمَثَّلُ لِي الْمَلَكُ رَجُلاً فَيُكَلِّمُنِي فَأَعِي مَا يَقُولُ

(Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), Sahih al-Bukhari)

This form is notable for its clarity and directness, making it easier for the Prophet to receive and understand the revelation.

☁️

Voice from the Sky

This form of revelation involved the Prophet (PBUH) hearing a voice from the sky. On some occasions, this was accompanied by a visual manifestation of Angel Gabriel, such as seeing him seated on a chair between the sky and the earth. This specific experience is notably linked to the resumption of revelation after the *Fatrah* period.

Prophet's Experience & Quote:

"While I was walking, I heard a voice from the sky. I looked up and saw the same angel who had visited me at the Cave of Hira sitting on a chair between the sky and the earth."

بَيْنَا أَنَا أَمْشِي سَمِعْتُ صَوْتًا مِنَ السَّمَاءِ، فَرَفَعْتُ بَصَرِي، فَإِذَا الْمَلَكُ الَّذِي جَاءَنِي بِحِرَاءٍ جَالِسٌ عَلَى كُرْسِيٍّ بَيْنَ السَّمَاءِ وَالأَرْضِ

(Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), Sahih al-Bukhari)

This experience could be initially frightening due to its grandeur and suddenness, prompting the Prophet to seek covering.

📖

Gabriel Teaching the Qur'an

Angel Gabriel had a regular practice of meeting Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) every night during the month of Ramadan. During these meetings, Gabriel would teach the Prophet the Qur'an, ensuring its accuracy, proper recitation, and preservation. This was a structured and continuous process of divine instruction.

Prophet's Experience & Quote:

"Gabriel used to meet him once every year during the month of Ramadan and would stay with him till the month was over, and Allah's Messenger (PBUH) used to recite the Qur'an before him."

كَانَ جِبْرِيلُ يَلْقَاهُ فِي كُلِّ لَيْلَةٍ مِنْ رَمَضَانَ فَيُدَارِسُهُ الْقُرْآنَ

(Sahih al-Bukhari)

This consistent review ensured the divine text was perfectly conveyed and memorized, highlighting Allah's guarantee for the Qur'an's preservation.
