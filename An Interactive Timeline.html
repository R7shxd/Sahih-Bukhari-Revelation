Pre-Revelation: Seclusion in Cave Hira

~6 months prior to first revelation

Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) developed a profound inclination towards solitary worship and contemplation in the Cave of Hira, preparing him spiritually for the momentous divine communication.

The First Revelation: 'Read!'

610 CE, Ramadan

Angel Gabriel appeared and commanded him to 'Read!' (Iqra'), marking the formal commencement of prophethood and the revelation of the first verses of the Qur'an.

Return to Khadija (RA)

Immediately after first revelation

Overwhelmed and frightened, the Prophet (PBUH) sought comfort from his wife, Khadija (RA), who immediately reassured him with profound faith and wisdom.

Waraqa bin Naufal's Confirmation

Soon after Khadija's reassurance

Khadija (RA) took the Prophet (PBUH) to her cousin, Waraqa, a Christian scholar, who confirmed the divine nature of the experience and identified the angel as Gabriel.

The Fatrah: Pause in Revelation

Period after Waraqa's passing

Following the initial revelations and Waraqa's passing, divine inspiration paused for a period, which deepened the Prophet's longing and prepared him for the next phase.

Resumption: 'Arise and Warn!'

After the Fatrah

Angel Gabriel reappeared, and the revelation of Surah Al-Muddaththir (Qur'an 74:1-5) marked the end of the Fatrah and a shift to public ministry.

Heraclius's Inquiry

Later in the Meccan Period

The Byzantine Emperor Heraclius questioned Abu Sufyan (then an opponent) about Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), leading to a significant external validation of his prophethood.
