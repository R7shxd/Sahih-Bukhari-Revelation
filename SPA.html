The Concept and Significance of Wahy (Divine Revelation)

This application explores the profound beginnings of Prophet Muhammad's (peace be upon him) prophethood, focusing on the initial divine revelations as detailed in Sahih al-Bukhari. Understanding these foundational events is key to appreciating the core of Islamic teachings.

The bedrock of Islamic faith and practice rests upon the concept of Wahy, or divine revelation. This term denotes the unique and authoritative communication from Allah to His chosen prophets, serving as the direct conduit for transmitting divine will, guidance, and scripture to humanity. Its paramount importance in the Islamic tradition cannot be overstated, as it is the very source from which the prophetic mission derives its legitimacy and the Qur'an its sacred origin.

Qur'anic Context:

"Verily, We have sent the revelation to you (O Muhammad) as We sent the revelation to Noah and the Prophets after him."

إِنَّا أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَيْكَ كَمَا أَوْحَيْنَا إِلَى نُوحٍ وَالنَّبِيِّينَ مِنْ بَعْدِهِ

(Qur'an 4:163)

This verse immediately places Prophet Muhammad within a venerable lineage of divinely inspired messengers, establishing a continuity of prophethood and suggesting a universal divine plan.

The Importance of Intention:

"The reward of deeds depends upon the intentions and every person will get the reward according to what he has intended."

إِنَّمَا الأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ، وَإِنَّمَا لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مَا نَوَى

(Sahih al-Bukhari 1)

The placement of this Hadith at the very beginning of Sahih al-Bukhari, after the introduction to revelation, suggests that the act of receiving and conveying divine revelation, and all actions based upon it, must be rooted in pure intention for Allah.

The Commencement of Prophethood: Events of Cave Hira

The formal commencement of Prophet Muhammad's (PBUH) prophethood is meticulously detailed through the events that transpired in the cave of Hira. This pivotal moment was preceded by a period of profound spiritual preparation.

Prior to the first revelation, Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) developed a deep inclination towards seclusion and solitary worship in the cave of Hira. This practice prepared him mentally and spiritually for the momentous divine communication.

The Encounter with Angel Gabriel:

Step 1: The Command - While in seclusion in Hira, the Angel Gabriel came to him and asked him to "Read" (اقْرَأْ - Iqra').

The Prophet (PBUH) replied, "I do not know how to read" (مَا أَنَا بِقَارِئٍ). This confirmed his illiteracy, making the subsequent revelation of scripture even more miraculous.

Step 2: The Pressing - The angel caught him forcefully and pressed him hard. This was repeated three times.

Each time, the Prophet (PBUH) stated his inability to read. This intense physical experience signified the gravity and undeniable reality of the encounter.

Step 3: The First Verses - After the third pressing, the angel recited the first verses of the Qur'an:

"'Read in the name of your Lord, who has created (all that exists), created man from a clot. Read! And your Lord is the Most Generous.'"

اقْرَأْ بِاسْمِ رَبِّكَ الَّذِي خَلَقَ. خَلَقَ الْإِنسَانَ مِنْ عَلَقٍ. اقْرَأْ وَرَبُّكَ الْأَكْرَمُ

(Qur'an 96:1-3)

These verses establish Allah's omnipotence, humanity's humble origin, and the importance of knowledge.

Step 4: The Return - Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) returned to Khadija, his heart beating severely, and said, "Cover me! Cover me!"

This highlighted the immense psychological and physical impact of the experience. Khadija's response was one of comfort and reassurance.

Pillars of Support: Khadija and Waraqa bin Naufal

The initial, overwhelming experience of divine revelation necessitated immediate and unwavering support for Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). His wife, Khadija bint Khuwailid, and her cousin, Waraqa bin Naufal, were instrumental in this critical phase.

Khadija bint Khuwailid (RA)

When the Prophet (PBUH) expressed his fear, Khadija reassured him with profound faith:

"Never! By Allah, Allah will never disgrace you. You keep good relations with your kith and kin, help the poor and the destitute, serve your guests generously and assist the deserving calamity-afflicted ones."

Her words provided crucial emotional and psychological anchoring, validating his experience through his exemplary character.

Khadija's support was not just emotional; she proactively sought external validation by taking the Prophet (PBUH) to her cousin Waraqa. This demonstrates her wisdom and practical approach in the face of an unprecedented event. Her unwavering belief in his integrity and the divine nature of his experience was the first human comfort and confirmation he received.

Waraqa bin Naufal

A Christian scholar knowledgeable in Hebrew scriptures, Waraqa identified the angel as Gabriel (Jibril), the same one sent to Moses (Musa AS):

"This is the same one who keeps the secrets (angel Gabriel) whom Allah had sent to Moses."

This linked the Prophet's (PBUH) experience to established prophetic tradition. Waraqa also foretold the opposition Muhammad (PBUH) would face and pledged his support.

Waraqa's confirmation provided significant theological validation from a respected external source. His prophecy, "Anyone (man) who came with something similar to what you have brought was treated with hostility," prepared the Prophet (PBUH) for future challenges, offering a sense of historical precedent and solidarity. This interaction bridged understanding with the People of the Book.

Modalities of Divine Inspiration (Wahy)

Divine revelation to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) manifested in various forms, each with distinct experiences. This multifaceted nature underscores its profound impact. Click on each form to learn more.

Good Dreams

Description: Came true like bright daylight; a gentle, preparatory introduction to Wahy.

Experience: Spiritual clarity, less overwhelming.

Narrated by 'Aisha (RA).

Ringing of a Bell (صلصلة الجرس)

Description: The hardest form of inspiration; an intense auditory and sensory experience.

Experience: Immense spiritual and physical burden. Prophet (PBUH) would sweat even on a very cold day. He would grasp what was inspired after the state passed.

Described by Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), observed by 'Aisha (RA).

Angel in Human Form

Description: Angel Gabriel would appear as a man and talk directly to the Prophet (PBUH).

Experience: Allowed for direct conversation and clear comprehension; more accessible reception.

Described by Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), narrated by 'Aisha (RA).

Voice from the Sky

Description: The Prophet (PBUH) would hear a voice and sometimes see Angel Gabriel (e.g., on a chair between sky and earth, marking resumption after Fatrah).

Experience: Could be initially frightening, prompting him to seek covering.

Narrated by Jabir bin 'Abdullah Al-Ansari (RA).

Gabriel Teaching the Qur'an

Description: Angel Gabriel used to meet the Prophet (PBUH) every night of Ramadan to teach him the Qur'an.

Experience: A regular, structured process of divine instruction and review, ensuring accuracy and preservation.

Narrated by Ibn 'Abbas (RA).

Divine Assurance for Preservation

The Prophet (PBUH) used to bear the revelation with great trouble and move his lips quickly. Allah then revealed:

"'Move not your tongue concerning (the Qur'an) to make haste therewith. It is for Us to collect it and to give you (O Muhammad) the ability to recite it... Then it is for Us (Allah) to make it clear to you.'"

لَا تُحَرِّكْ بِهِ لِسَانَكَ لِتَعْجَلَ بِهِ. إِنَّ عَلَيْنَا جَمْعَهُ وَقُرْآنَهُ. فَإِذَا قَرَأْنَاهُ فَاتَّبِعْ قُرْآنَهُ. ثُمَّ إِنَّ عَلَيْنَا بَيَانَهُ

(Qur'an 75:16-19)

This established a divine guarantee for the Qur'an's preservation and clarity, a cornerstone of Islamic faith.

The Interlude and Resumption: Period of Fatrah

Following the initial revelations and Waraqa's passing, divine inspiration paused for a period known as Fatrah. This interlude, though initially distressing, served a crucial purpose.

The Pause (Fatrah)

This period created a longing for divine connection within the Prophet (PBUH), allowing him to consolidate the initial experience and appreciate the gift of Wahy. It was not abandonment but a time for spiritual strengthening.

The Resumption

The Prophet (PBUH) recounted seeing the same angel from Hira on a chair between sky and earth. Though initially frightened, this signaled the end of Fatrah. Allah then revealed:

"'O you (Muhammad)! wrapped up in garments! Arise and warn... and desert the idols.'"

يَا أَيُّهَا الْمُدَّثِّرُ. قُمْ فَأَنذِرْ. وَرَبَّكَ فَكَبِّرْ. وَثِيَابَكَ فَطَهِّرْ. وَالرُّجْزَ فَاهْجُرْ

(Qur'an 74:1-5)

Shift in Mission

This marked a fundamental shift:
Before Fatrah (Surah Al-Alaq): Personal command to "Read," focusing on receiving the message.
After Fatrah (Surah Al-Muddaththir): Public command to "Arise and warn," initiating active proselytization and confronting idolatry.

This highlighted the progression from individual preparation to societal transformation. Revelation then started coming strongly, frequently, and regularly.

Heraclius's Inquiry: External Testimony

A compelling external validation of Prophet Muhammad's (PBUH) prophethood comes from the detailed interaction between Heraclius, the Byzantine Emperor, and Abu Sufyan (then an opponent of Islam). Heraclius, a learned ruler, posed incisive questions. Click each question to reveal the answer and Heraclius's deduction.

Heraclius's Conclusion

Based on Abu Sufyan's truthful answers, Heraclius, with his scriptural knowledge, deduced Muhammad (PBUH) possessed prophetic characteristics. He stated:

"If what you have said is true, he will very soon occupy this place underneath my feet... if I could reach him definitely, I would go immediately to meet him and if I were with him, I would certainly wash his feet."

This powerful endorsement from a foreign, non-Muslim ruler significantly authenticates the Prophet's (PBUH) mission.

Enduring Significance and Foundational Impact

The early revelations to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), meticulously documented, hold profound and enduring significance. They establish the divine origin of the Islamic message, detail the methods of inspiration, highlight the Prophet's exemplary character, and lay the groundwork for the Qur'an's preservation.

The interplay of intense personal experiences (like in Cave Hira) with external validations (from Khadija, Waraqa, and even adversaries like Abu Sufyan via Heraclius) creates a robust framework affirming his prophethood. The divine assurance for the Qur'an's preservation (75:16-19) is a cornerstone of faith.

The transition from the personal command "Read!" to the public mandate "Arise and warn!" after the Fatrah period signifies the shift from individual spiritual preparation to a universal call to guidance. The human elements of fear and the need for reassurance make the prophetic journey relatable and his steadfastness even more remarkable.

These foundational narratives are not just historical accounts; they are a continuing source of guidance, inspiration, and affirmation for believers, forming the bedrock of Islamic faith and practice.
